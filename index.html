Jes Ford, PhD

Data Science Postdoc

I am a Moore/Sloan Data Science and WRF Innovation in Data Science Postdoctoral Fellow in the eScience Institute at the University of Washington. My work focuses on using machine learning and other statistical techniques to characterize galaxy clusters in large astronomical surveys.

Science & Research

I work primarily in the field of cosmology - the study of the universe as a whole - with a focus on galaxy clusters and gravitational lensing. I am particularly interested in data science techniques, and now that I have joined the eScience Institute, I am branching out into different realms of astronomy for the chance to apply interesting algorithms and machine learning to problems in my field. See my research page.

The 3D-MF Galaxy Cluster Catalog contains over 18,000 cluster candidates compiled from the CFHTLenS survey, and is publicly available. Download the catalog and learn more about it.
